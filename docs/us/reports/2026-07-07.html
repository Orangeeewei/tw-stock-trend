Glossary (plain words)

Sector rotation
Money rotates among the 11 GICS sectors. This report tracks where money is flowing in, then hunts the strongest names in that sector that are at or near a breakout.
Off 60-day high
Distance below the 3-month high. Closer to 0% means nearer a breakout — the strongest setup; this strategy only takes names at or near the prior high.
Volume surge
5-day average volume vs the 20-day average; above 1.2× means buyers are showing up.
Momentum turn
A positive 5-day return that also beats the sector — confirmation the name is strengthening with the group.
60-day moving average
Average cost of the past three months' buyers. S&P 500 above it = bull regime; below = bear. This is the report's master switch.
vs the Taiwan edition
The US lacks Taiwan's daily institutional flow and monthly revenue disclosures, so scoring here uses price/volume structure only. Prices are dividend-adjusted automatically.
Exit ladder (Wang's two-tier)
Exits follow the moving averages: trim half on a break of the 5-day MA, exit fully on a break of the 10-day MA, with the 10-day-low close as a third line. The prices under the Close column are these reference levels.
Unfilled downside gap ⚠️
The price gapped down and left an un-traded blank that hasn't filled back; until it does, that zone is overhead resistance and the trend isn't truly turning up.
False breakout ⚠️
It made a 60-day high (apparent breakout) but the close fell back below the 5-day MA and the neckline, with a downside gap after — the breakout may be fake.
